Blog

Our Blog allows you to write about topics and to share your opinion, as long as it is about the Codidact software or the community. It's also used for official announcements of the Codidact team.

Blog posts are made at the discretion of their author. They can't be edited by anyone else, except for our moderators to enforce our policies. Here's a list of our top blog posts. Take a bit of time and click through them to find out what we see as good blog posts.

This is a community about Meta, so we expect blog posts to be about the usage of the Codidact software or the community policies. Examples are ...

  • how to use the Codidact software.
  • an analysis you did with our communities.
  • which changes you'd like to have.
  • inviting discussion around the Codidact software or community.

Some posts are not a good fit for the blog platform. Please avoid writing posts, that are ...

  • not constructive rants that don't adhere to our Code of Conduct.
  • not about the Codidact software or community.

Wiki posts can be voted upon and – when they don't fulfill our quality requirements – hidden until someone fix them. You can only edit your own blog posts.

Comments

Comments are supplemental. Therefore they are only shown to you, when you click the "see X comments" button. You can use them to discuss parts of an post, that you think are wrong, or to give feedback. You can also use them to suggest improvemens or to ask for clarification from the author.

Behavior and Moderation

We expect every user to behave nicely and to adhere to our Code of Conduct.

If you see something that's unfriendly, you can flag it for moderator attention. Our community-elected moderator team will take a look at it and act accordingly.

Most moderation activity – especially regarding post quality – will be done by normal community members, though. Everyone starts with a trust level of 0 and as they provide useful content, they progress up to trust level 6. Each level grants more moderation powers and reduces some security limits. So get started and write a post to earn your first trust level.
