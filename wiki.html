Wiki

Our Wiki is a collection of canonical posts and often-asked questions.

Wiki posts can be edited by anyone with a little bit positive activity. However, they should only be created sparingly and only when needed. When in doubt, get some community agreement before creating one. Here's a list of our top wiki entries. Take a bit of time and click through them to find out what we see as good wiki entries.

This is a community about Meta, so we expect wiki posts to be about the usage of the Codidact software or the community policies. Examples are ...

  • how to use the Codidact software.
  • that some behavior is or isn't acceptable here.
  • what the community policies for the whole Codidact network are.

Some posts are not a good fit for the wiki platform. Please avoid writing posts, that are ...

  • not constructive and should rather be questions.
  • wrong or just represent one side of a discussion.
  • not about the Codidact software or community.
  • about policies that have not been accepted or even rejected by the community.

Wiki posts can be voted upon and – when they don't fulfill our quality requirements – hidden until someone fix them. Once you gain some experience you can edit all wiki posts.

Comments

Comments are supplemental. Therefore they are only shown to you, when you click the "see X comments" button. You can use them to discuss parts of an entry, that you think are wrong, or to give feedback. You can also use them to suggest improvemens.

Behavior and Moderation

We expect every user to behave nicely and to adhere to our Code of Conduct.

If you see something that's unfriendly, you can flag it for moderator attention. Our community-elected moderator team will take a look at it and act accordingly.

Most moderation activity – especially regarding post quality – will be done by normal community members, though. Everyone starts with a trust level of 0 and as they provide useful content, they progress up to trust level 6. Each level grants more moderation powers and reduces some security limits. So get started and write a post to earn your first trust level.
